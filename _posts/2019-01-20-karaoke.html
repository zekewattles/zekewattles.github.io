How do you capture content for making the perfect deepfakes in a way that’s fun?

Karaoke.

Category: Interactive installation, motion graphics, video
Instructor: Jennifer Rodenhouse
Year: 2019

Can deepfakes be a design tool?

Deepfakes are videos that uses artificial intelligence and facial pattern recognition to map one person’s face onto another. To create a convincing transfer, all you need two video sources, with one face each, in a variety of angles and expressions. Interviews, speeches, and lectures are best, but what about a passionate karaoke performance of “Heroes” by David Bowie — what if you could, through technology, become your heroes, too?

Requirements for Making a Good Deepfake Video

2 videos, ~1-2 min each

Clearly visible faces

Multiple expressions

Multiple angles

Concept: Heroes — David Bowie

Based on the above criteria, David Bowie’s iconic “Heroes” music video is a perfect candidate for deepfaking.

Set-Up

  • Greenscreen
  • Spotlight
  • Short-throw projector
  • Camera tripod
  • iPhone (as camera)
  • Stool (some used it, some didn’t)

User Test 1: Heroes — David Bowie

For the first test I created a karaoke video for ”Heroes” and used AfterEffects to move the video around on the wall in an attempt to capture more head angles. Afterwards, my first tester, Mikiko, asked: “can I pick my own song?”

User Test 2: Choose Your Own Song

This time I projected a YouTube window and let people pick whatever they wanted. There was no significant improvement by using moving video over normal karaoke video — people naturally move to the music. And it’s more fun.

Learnings

Having fun is the most important thing. Karaoke works best when people have the freedom to express themselves.

  • People naturally move their head when they get into a song.
  • The capture result still meets requirements for a good AI deepfake.
  • The audio isn’t important, only the angles and expressions.
