An installation and video piece where karaoke becomes a way to collect data making deepfakes. Work in progress.

Category: Interactive installation, motion graphics, video
Instructor: Jennifer Rodenhouse
Year: 2019

Can deepfakes be a design tool?

After extensive research into the process of creating deepfakes, I concluded that karaoke is the perfect vehicle for collecting data to create deepfakes with. Deepfake Karaoke is an installation designed for the creation of a piece of video art: a collection of everyday people singing “Heroes” from David Bowie, then deepfaked into the original music video. While most deepfake videos focus on grafting one famous face onto another, Deepfake Karaoke takes a more democratic approaching, giving everyday people the opportunity to be heroes... just for one day.

Data collection

To make a good deepfake, you need multiple facial angles. A brief “calibration” video ensures this data is collected.
