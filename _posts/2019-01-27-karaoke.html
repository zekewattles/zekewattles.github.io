An installation and video piece where karaoke becomes a tool for deepfake data collection. Work in progress.

Category: Interactive installation, motion graphics, video
Instructor: Jennifer Rodenhouse
Year: 2019

Can deepfakes be a design tool?

I‘ve been fascinated with the phenomenon of deepfakes. For the unfamiliar, a deepfake is a video that uses artificial intelligence to map one person’s face onto another. To create a good one, all you really need is two video sources, with one face each, in a variety of angles and expressions. But could there be a side of deepfakes that’s less scary, Black Mirror-esque? Yes. A passionate karaoke performance is the perfect vehicle for capturing deepfake data.

Initially, I learned the software by experimenting with deepfaking myself into successful design and tech “heroes” like Tim Cook and Elon Musk. I wondered if there could be a deeper side to this idea of deepfaking — what does it mean to “embody” someone? To take their voice?

Creating content

I chose the song “Heroes” by David Bowie as my initial deepfaking video. Its music video is a perfect candidate — it features Bowie and Bowie only, with plenty of close-up shots. Thematically, the lyrics are a perfect fit — what if you could, through technology, become your heroes, too?

Heroes — David Bowie, karaoke edition, for data collection.
Heroes — David Bowie, the original destination video for deepfaking. Note the format: one person, plenty of closeups. It’s not HD, but neither are most deepfakes, so I chose to embrace it.
